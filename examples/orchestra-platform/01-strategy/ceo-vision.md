# Orchestra Platform — CEO Vision

## Problem Statement

Mid-market engineering teams — those with 50 to 200 developers — are stuck in a productivity trap. They spend roughly 30% of their engineering hours on infrastructure toil: provisioning environments, wiring up CI/CD pipelines, managing secrets, and maintaining internal tooling. That's nearly one-third of every sprint burned on work that delivers zero customer value. These teams are too large to operate without a platform, yet too small to justify a dedicated platform engineering team. The result is burnout, slow feature delivery, and frustrated developers who joined to build products, not maintain Jenkins.

## Solution

Orchestra is Backstage-as-a-Service. We take Spotify's open-source developer portal — already trusted by thousands of engineering organizations — and deliver it as a fully managed, production-ready cloud offering. Teams sign up, connect their Git repositories, and get a complete service catalog, software templates, and plugin ecosystem within hours, not months. No platform team required. No infrastructure to manage. No YAML archaeology.

## Target Market

There are approximately 8,000 companies globally with engineering teams in the 50–200 developer range. That represents a $2.4 billion total addressable market, growing at 18% annually as more organizations adopt platform engineering practices.

## Business Model

B2B SaaS with transparent, predictable pricing: $29 per developer per month plus a $500 monthly platform fee that includes 5 custom templates, SSO, and premium support. Customers can be live on a 14-day free trial with no credit card required.

## Competitive Moat

Our moat is two-fold. First, an open-source core (Backstage) creates trust and community adoption; our managed cloud adds the operational reliability, security, and support that enterprises need. Second, our plugin marketplace generates network effects — each new plugin increases the platform's value for every customer, and plugin developers earn revenue share, incentivizing ecosystem growth. This is the same playbook that made WordPress and Shopify dominant.
