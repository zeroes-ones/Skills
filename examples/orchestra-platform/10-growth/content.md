# Content Strategy

## Blog Program

Technical blog published at `orchestra.dev/blog` with a 2x/week cadence (Tuesdays and Thursdays, 9am ET). Content pillars: tutorial posts (step-by-step guides using Orchestra), comparison articles (Orchestra vs. Backstage, Port, Cortex), and case studies (customer outcomes with metrics). Each post targets 1,500–2,500 words with code snippets, screenshots, and a clear CTA (free trial signup or template execution).

## Content Clusters

Three thematic clusters designed for topical authority in search:

**IDP Fundamentals**: "What Is an Internal Developer Platform?", "Build vs. Buy Your IDP", "Measuring Developer Productivity with DORA Metrics", "The Golden Path Pattern Explained." This cluster targets top-of-funnel search traffic and establishes Orchestra as a thought leader in the platform engineering space.

**Platform Engineering in Practice**: "How We Reduced Onboarding from 3 Weeks to 15 Minutes," "Plugin-Driven Architecture at Orchestra," "Multi-Region Deployment Patterns for SaaS Platforms," "Zero-Trust Networking for Internal Platforms." Mid-funnel content demonstrating technical depth and real-world implementation.

**Developer Experience**: "Why Your Developers Hate Your Internal Tools," "Designing a Developer CLI That Engineers Actually Use," "The Role of Design Systems in Developer Portals," "Feedback Loops: From Support Ticket to Product Feature." Practitioner-focused content that resonates with the target audience (platform engineers, DevOps leads, CTOs).

## Distribution Channels

- **dev.to**: Cross-post under the `#platformengineering` and `#devops` tags, published 3 days after the blog (canonical URL retained).
- **HackerNews**: Manual submission by the author (no automated posting) with a "Show HN" tag for product updates.
- **Reddit**: r/devops, r/platformengineering, r/kubernetes — posts framed as discussion starters, not link dumps.
- **Newsletter**: Bi-weekly email digest (every other Wednesday). 500 subscribers at launch (waitlist + beta users). Format: 1 featured article + 2 curated links + product update. Open rate target: 35%.

## Content Operations

All posts authored in Markdown, stored in the website repo under `content/blog/`. Review process: draft → peer review (technical accuracy) → editorial review (clarity, SEO, voice) → publish. Asana project board tracks each post through the pipeline with a 2-week lead time.
