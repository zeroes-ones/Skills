# NIST AI RMF 1.0 — Reference

## Overview

NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0, January 2023) provides a structured
approach to managing AI risks across the AI lifecycle. It is voluntary (unlike the EU AI
Act) but is the most comprehensive AI risk framework available.

## Core Functions

### GOVERN

Organizational governance of AI risk. Policies, accountability, culture, workforce
diversity, third-party risk management, and integration with enterprise risk management.

**Key artifacts:** AI risk management policy, AI risk officer designation, AI inventory
with risk categorization, third-party AI risk assessment process.

### MAP

Context recognition — understanding the AI system's purpose, stakeholders, legal/regulatory
context, and potential impacts. Categorizing AI systems by risk level.

**Key artifacts:** AI system inventory, use case documentation, stakeholder impact
assessment, trustworthiness characteristics mapping (valid & reliable, safe, secure &
resilient, accountable & transparent, explainable & interpretable, privacy-enhanced,
fair with harmful bias managed).

### MEASURE

Quantitative and qualitative assessment of AI risks. Trustworthiness evaluation,
adversarial testing, fairness metrics, explainability assessment, and ongoing monitoring.

**Key artifacts:** Security assessment results, fairness metrics, explainability reports,
robustness testing results, monitoring dashboards.

### MANAGE

Risk response — accept, mitigate, transfer, or avoid. Residual risk acceptance,
continuous monitoring, incident response, and feedback loops for improvement.

**Key artifacts:** Risk treatment plan, residual risk sign-off, monitoring alerts,
incident response playbook, post-incident review records.

## Trustworthiness Characteristics

AI systems should be: valid & reliable, safe, secure & resilient, accountable &
transparent, explainable & interpretable, privacy-enhanced, and fair with harmful bias
managed. These characteristics are interdependent — improving one may degrade another
(e.g., explainability vs accuracy trade-offs).
