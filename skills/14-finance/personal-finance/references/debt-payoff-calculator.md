# Debt Payoff Calculator: Avalanche vs Snowball

## The Math

**Avalanche method:** Pay minimum on all debts, direct all extra money to highest APR debt first.
**Snowball method:** Pay minimum on all debts, direct all extra money to smallest balance first.

Avalanche always minimizes total interest paid. Snowball provides psychological wins that improve adherence (paying off first debt faster).

## Example Comparison

| Method | Payoff Order | Total Interest | Time | Psychological Benefit |
|--------|-------------|----------------|------|----------------------|
| Avalanche | CC (25%), Loan (8%), Student (4%) | $2,847 | 34 months | Best mathematically |
| Snowball | Loan ($3K), CC ($10K), Student ($20K) | $3,412 | 34 months | First win in 8 months vs 22 |

**Recommendation:** If snowball costs <$500 extra AND user has history of abandoning financial plans, recommend snowball. Otherwise, avalanche.

## Debt Payoff Priority Matrix

| Debt Type | APR | Priority | Action |
|-----------|-----|----------|--------|
| Payday loan | 300-600% | CRITICAL | Pay immediately, never use again |
| Credit card | 20-30% | CRITICAL | Avalanche target, consider balance transfer |
| Personal loan | 8-36% | HIGH | Pay after credit cards |
| Private student loan | 5-13% | MEDIUM | Consider refinancing |
| Auto loan | 4-8% | MEDIUM | Pay on schedule |
| Federal student loan | 3-7% | LOW | Minimum payments, explore IDR/PSLF |
| Mortgage | 3-7% | LOWEST | Pay on schedule, tax-deductible interest |
