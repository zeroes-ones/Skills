# Investment Policy Statement Template

An IPS is your investing constitution -- it prevents emotional decisions during market volatility.

## Section 1: Investment Philosophy

```
I believe that markets are efficient and that low-cost, broad-market index funds
provide the best risk-adjusted returns for long-term investors. I do not attempt
to time the market or pick individual winning stocks. I accept that markets will
decline 30-50% multiple times during my investing lifetime, and I commit to
staying invested through these declines.
```

**Fund Selection Criteria:**
- Expense ratio < 0.15%
- Broad market exposure (not sector/thematic)
- Passive (index) fund, not actively managed
- No loads, no 12b-1 fees

## Section 5: Rebalancing Rules

- Rebalance when any asset class deviates >5 percentage points from target
- Use new contributions to buy underweight assets (tax-efficient)
- In taxable accounts: rebalance with dividends + new money only (avoid capital gains)
- Rebalance across ALL accounts as one portfolio (asset location optimization)

## Section 6: Rules I Will Not Break

- [ ] I will not sell during a market decline of any magnitude
- [ ] I will not buy individual stocks with more than 5% of my portfolio
- [ ] I will not take investment advice from social media, TV, or friends without independent verification
- [ ] I will not check my portfolio more than once per quarter
- [ ] I will not change my asset allocation based on market predictions
- [ ] I will increase contributions during market declines (stocks are on sale)

## Section 7: Review Schedule

- **Quarterly:** Check asset allocation, rebalance if >5% drift
- **Annually:** Review goals, update contribution amounts, tax-loss harvest
- **Life events:** Revisit entire IPS (marriage, children, job change, inheritance)
