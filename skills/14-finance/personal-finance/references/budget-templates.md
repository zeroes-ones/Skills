## Emergency Fund Tiers

| Tier | Amount | Where to Hold | When to Use |
|------|--------|---------------|-------------|
| Tier 1: Immediate | 1 month expenses | Checking account (same bank) | Same-day emergencies: car repair, urgent medical |
| Tier 2: Short-term | 2 months expenses | HYSA (separate bank, 2-day transfer) | Job loss, major repair, unexpected large expense |
| Tier 3: Extended | 3-9 months expenses | Money market fund, CD ladder, I-bonds (after 1yr) | Extended job loss, sabbatical, major medical event |
| Tier 4: Deep reserve | Beyond 12 months | Taxable brokerage (conservative: 30/70 stocks/bonds) | Extreme scenarios: multi-year job loss, disability |

## Annualized Expense Calculation

To convert irregular expenses to monthly budget items:

```
Annual expenses / 12 = monthly sinking fund contribution

Examples:
  Car insurance ($1,200/yr):    $100/month
  Holiday gifts ($600/yr):       $50/month
  Annual subscriptions ($240):   $20/month
  Car maintenance ($800/yr):     $67/month
  Home maintenance (1% of value): $X/month
  Property tax ($3,600/yr):     $300/month
```

This prevents "surprise" expenses -- they are planned.
