## Life Insurance: Needs Analysis

```
Immediate Needs:
  Funeral costs:                    $10,000-$15,000
  Outstanding debts:                $__________ (mortgage, loans, credit cards)
  Emergency fund for survivors:     $__________ (6-12 months expenses)
  Children's education:             $__________ (college costs x number of kids)

Ongoing Needs:
  Annual living expenses:           $__________
  Years of support needed:          __________
  Total income replacement:         $__________ (annual expenses x years)

Total Life Insurance Need:          $__________
Current Coverage:                   $__________
GAP:                                $__________
```

**Rule of thumb:** 10-12x annual income. More accurate: DIME method (Debt + Income + Mortgage + Education).

## Term vs Whole Life Comparison

| | Term Life | Whole Life |
|---|-----------|------------|
| Monthly cost ($500K, age 35, healthy) | $25-40/month | $400-600/month |
| Coverage period | 10-30 years | Lifetime |
| Cash value component | None | Yes (grows slowly) |
| Investment return | N/A | ~1.5-3.5% after fees |
| Best for | Almost everyone with dependents | Estate tax planning ($13M+ estates) |

**Recommendation:** Buy level term (20-30 years), invest the premium difference in index funds.

## Disability Insurance

```
Your most valuable asset is your future income:
  Age 30, $80K income, 35 years to retirement = $2.8M in future earnings

Group LTD (employer): Typically 60% of base salary, taxable benefit
Individual DI: Covers bonus/commission too, tax-free benefit, portable

Target: 60-70% of after-tax income, own-occupation definition
Wait for elimination period: 90 days (use emergency fund)
Benefit period: to age 65-67

Coverage gap: Group LTD covers $__________, Individual DI adds $__________
```

## Umbrella Insurance

```
Net worth (excluding primary home equity): $__________
Current liability coverage (auto + home):  $__________
Umbrella recommendation: $1M-$2M coverage (cost: $150-$300/year per $1M)

Rule: Umbrella coverage >= Net worth minus primary home equity
  (protects assets from lawsuits exceeding auto/home liability limits)
```

## When to Self-Insure

Self-insure when: (1) you can afford the loss without lifestyle impact, (2) the premium exceeds expected value significantly.

| Insurance | When to Drop |
|-----------|-------------|
| Collision/comprehensive (old car) | Car value < $5,000, premium >10% of value |
| Extended warranty | Always (self-insure, warranties are high-margin products) |
| Pet insurance | Can afford $5-10K emergency (self-insure with pet emergency fund) |
| Cell phone insurance | Can afford replacement ($500-1000), premium >$15/month |
| Rental car insurance | Credit card covers it, or auto policy extends to rentals |
