# What Good Looks Like — Full Quality Standard

The threat intelligence program operates as a continuous feedback loop — raw data flows from collection through processing to analysis, producing actionable intelligence at strategic, operational, and tactical levels within hours, not weeks. Every threat actor tracked has a living MITRE ATT&CK profile with mapped TTPs, diamond model annotations, and confidence-scored IOCs updated in real-time. Intelligence is automatically disseminated to the SOC (tactical IOCs for SIEM correlation), to security engineering (operational TTPs for detection engineering), and to executive leadership (strategic threat landscape briefings). The CTI platform (OpenCTI or MISP) is the single source of truth, with STIX 2.1 as the canonical format and automated TAXII feeds to all consumers. Threat hunts are hypothesis-driven, documented, and continuously refined based on intelligence gaps. The organization detects novel threats days before vendor signatures are available because the CTI team mapped the adversary's TTPs, not just their IOCs.
