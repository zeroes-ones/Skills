# Empirical Evidence: Evaluating Prototype Result Quality

## Evidence Quality Classification

### HIGH
- Hypothesis was clearly confirmed or disproved.
- Results are unambiguous and repeatable.
- The prototype exercised the key differentiator between approaches.
- **Action:** Decision can be made with confidence.

### MEDIUM
- Results are directionally clear but with caveats.
- Some variables were not controlled (e.g., local vs production environment).
- The prototype tested a simplified version of the real scenario.
- **Action:** Decision can be made with caveats documented. Plan follow-up validation.

### LOW
- Results are inconclusive or contradictory.
- The prototype was too narrow to answer the question.
- Confounding variables prevent interpretation.
- **Action:** Do NOT make the decision from this prototype. Re-scope with tighter question.

## Confounded Variable Detection
Ask: "What else could explain these results besides the hypothesis being true/false?"
List alternative explanations. If any are plausible, the evidence quality drops.
