# Branch Resolution Criteria

## Status Definitions

### RESOLVED
The decision has been made with clear rationale that references evidence, constraints, or explicit assumptions.
- Exit check: "Can I explain this decision to someone not in the room and they would agree it's the only reasonable choice given the constraints?"

### DEFERRED
The decision cannot be made now but has a concrete resolution plan.
- Must include: resolution date, responsible person, information needed, and what changes if the information is unavailable by the date.
- Anti-pattern: "We'll figure it out later." This is avoidance, not deferral.

### ASSUMED
Treated as true for now, but flagged for verification.
- Must include: what evidence would validate the assumption, what changes if it's false, and when verification will happen.
- Anti-pattern: Treating assumptions as facts without acknowledgment.

### UNRESOLVED
Cannot decide with available information and cannot defer. Escalate to parent skill.

## Resolution Quality Checklist
- [ ] Decision includes rationale (not just conclusion)
- [ ] Rationale references specific evidence, constraint, or assumption
- [ ] Alternative considered and rejected with reason
- [ ] "What would make this wrong?" can be answered
