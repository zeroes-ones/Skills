# Interview Pacing: When to Push, When to Pause

## Pacing Guidelines

### Push (Ask the next question immediately)
- The user gave a clear, specific answer with rationale.
- The answer opens a new sub-branch that needs exploration.
- The user is engaged and the answers are getting deeper.

### Pause (Allow reflection)
- The user hesitates or says "I need to think about that."
- The question reveals a conflict with a previously held assumption.
- The answer requires data the user doesn't have at hand (mark DEFERRED if > 2 min to retrieve).

### Stop (The branch is exhausted)
- Three passes over the same point with no new information.
- The user is repeating themselves or becoming frustrated.
- The remaining uncertainty is residual — not reducible by more questioning.

## Decision Fatigue Detection
- Sign: Answers get shorter and more dismissive.
- Sign: User defers decisions they previously engaged with.
- Sign: "Whatever you think is best" (abdication, not decision).
- Response: "I'm sensing decision fatigue. We have [N] branches remaining. Should we pause, defer the remaining to [date], or push through [M] more critical branches?"
