# Anti-Rationalization Table: Excuse vs Reality

| Excuse | Reality | Real-World Outcome | Prevention |
|--------|---------|--------------------|------------|
| "We don't have time to explore" | You don't have time NOT to explore. Exploration takes hours; rebuilding takes weeks. | A CRM integration skipped exploration → wrong integration. Rebuild cost: $380K. | Show the cost of skipping. Make the multiplier visible. |
| "We already know what to build" | Knowing WHAT without knowing WHY produces features nobody uses. 64% of features rarely/never used (Standish Group). | AI recommendation engine: $180K dev cost, $1,200 incremental revenue. | Validate problem with evidence before building. |
| "The stakeholder just wants it done" | The stakeholder wants it to WORK. A stakeholder who resists exploration will blame you when it fails. | VP demanded skip exploration on reporting tool. Wrong reports. 6-month delay. | Show stakeholder the anti-rationalization table. |
| "We'll figure it out during implementation" | Implementation is the most expensive time to figure things out. Discovery cost multiplies 10x at each phase. | Design flaw discovered during UAT → $95K late-stage fix. | Enforce exploration gate. Implementation starts after design approval. |
| "It's just a simple feature" | "Simple" is the most expensive word in software. Every production outage started as a simple change. | "Simple" database migration → 4-hour outage, $250K revenue loss. | Simple doesn't mean risk-free. Apply the checklist regardless of perceived simplicity. |
| "The team has done this before" | Past experience creates blind spots. Assuming sameness misses the one difference that matters. | "We've done auth before" → missed regulatory requirement, $150K compliance fine. | Fresh eyes. Every project gets full exploration. No experience exceptions. |
| "Industry standard" / "Best practice" | These justifications support any decision. They answer "what do others do?" not "what should WE do?" | "Industry standard" monolith → wrong for their scale, $200K migration to microservices. | Require context-specific reasoning. "Best practice for WHO?" |
| "We can iterate later" | Iteration only works if users stick around. A confusing first experience loses users permanently. | Confusing checkout flow: 14% abandonment, $180K lost lifetime value. | Paper prototypes cost $0. Test before building. |
